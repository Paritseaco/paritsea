# Paritsea Identity, Voice, and Visual System

Status: Canonical
Direction: Trace / Margin / Revision

## Identity

Paritsea should feel like a living intellectual record made by a recognisable author and governed well enough to outlast an individual publishing moment. Its memorable quality is not decoration; it is the visibility of how a thought moved, changed, and became more formal.

## Voice modes

- First person: Journal observations, uncertainty, revision rationale, and decisions not yet made.
- Signed authorial voice: Concept records and Framework introductions.
- Governed editorial voice: definitions, status, version, provenance, scope, licensing, and navigation.
- Institutional application voice: belongs to SE Ocean, not Paritsea.

The voice may be sharp, unresolved, or dissenting. It must still name its evidence, limits, and consequences. Avoid universal claims, hot takes, motivational language, mystery used as status, and personal mythology.

## Visual grammar

The interface uses traces, margins, revision marks, relationship lines, open and closed nodes, and document states. These elements explain provenance or status; they are not ornamental infographics.

### Mark and wordmark

The primary mark is a trace: an open path moving through observations and arriving at a formalized point without implying that every path must finish. The wordmark is an authored imprint rather than a corporate masthead. The mark is reserved for the favicon, document stamp, and provenance diagrams.

### Color roles

- Paper: the reading ground.
- Ink: authored and canonical text.
- Graphite: metadata and supporting language.
- Oxide: authorial intervention, revision, and active focus.
- Blue-green: an external or applied context, never a decorative secondary accent.
- Status is communicated with text and shape as well as color.

Light and dark modes share the same semantic roles. Both must meet WCAG 2.2 AA.

### Typography

- English editorial and display: Newsreader.
- English UI: Inter.
- Metadata: JetBrains Mono.
- Thai: Sarabun in every context.

English long-form targets 18px with 1.75 line height. Thai long-form targets 17–18px with at least 1.9 line height. Thai headings use weight 300, normal style, and no letter spacing. Thai supporting text never falls below 14px.

### Layout

Long-form text remains between 680 and 720px. The surrounding frame may expand to 1760px on 4K displays so provenance, outline, and relationship information can use the additional space without widening the reading measure.

### Motion and imagery

Motion is limited to 160–240ms state transitions, relationship reveals, and navigational feedback. Reduced-motion preferences disable non-essential movement. Paritsea does not use stock illustration. Images are source artifacts, annotated excerpts, documentary material, or functional diagrams with captions and provenance.
