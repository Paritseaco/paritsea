## What Paritsea is

Paritsea is a layered thinking system built on one core movement: **seeing → structuring → doing.**

It is not a brand. Not a consulting framework. Not a content platform.

It is a way of reading situations — people, organizations, systems, lives — that does not stop at what is visible. It reads the layer beneath, where what is hidden usually explains what is seen.

---

## The core lens

**What lies beneath the surface usually explains everything we don't understand. Paritsea reads that layer.**

This lens is not built from theory or classrooms. It is built from observing people in real situations — closely, across many dimensions — until what others walk past becomes visible.

The lens sees:
- The human cost inside systems that appear functional
- The wounds inside relationships that appear simply "broken"
- The missing thing inside organizations that appear successful
- The complexity beneath problems that appear simple

---

## The three-layer pipeline (locked — foundational)

| Layer | Page | What it does |
|-------|------|-------------|
| **Seeing** | Journal | Observation made legible — problems named before they are governed |
| **Structuring** | System (Framework / Protocol / Standard) | Observation formalized into governed knowledge |
| **Doing** | Implementation | Governed knowledge applied in reality |

These are not sequential steps a reader must follow. They are the natural movement of thought from raw experience to structured understanding to applied use. A reader enters at any layer; the architecture makes the movement *available*, not required.

## The seeing → structuring → doing principle (applied)

### In content decisions
Every piece of content sits at exactly one altitude. Nothing borrows from a neighbor's altitude to seem richer. An observation does not become a rule until it is formalized. A rule does not become an application until it runs in reality.

### In navigation decisions
The pipeline is expressed in the architecture (Journal / System / Implementation) but never forced on a reader as a sequence. A reader enters anywhere; the architecture makes the direction *available*.

### In design decisions
Depth is always pulled, never pushed. Surface users may rest in Journal without ever entering System. The layering is for those who go deeper — it never penalizes those who stay on the surface.

### In CTA decisions
No page optimizes for conversion. The one exception is Implementation, which is the only page where a builder CTA is appropriate — because a builder is already in the doing layer, and a CTA there is honest, not extractive.

## Language and terminology governance

### Two-model language system (D-028)

Paritsea runs two complementary language models on different axes:

- **Model A — Structural architecture (IA truth):** Journal → System → Implementation. *Where content lives.* This is the structural truth; it wins any apparent conflict.
- **Model B — Language exposure overlay (`language-hierarchy.md`):** Entry → Gateway → Core. *How heavy the words may be at a given reading depth.* Applied on top of Model A.

Pipeline terms (Journal / System / Implementation / Framework) are **Entry-layer** vocabulary — instantly understandable, allowed in nav and onboarding. Core ontology vocabulary (Cognitive Pattern, Recursive Thought Structure, Perception Architecture) is reserved for deep content and framework explanation — **never** nav or onboarding. The `language-hierarchy.md` vocabulary lists are **prescriptive**.

### Navigation is fully English (D-029)

**All navigation labels are English in both TH and EN. No Thai navigation terms.**

| Nav label | EN | TH (nav) |
|-----------|----|---------|
| Layer 1 | Journal | Journal |
| Layer 2 | System | System |
| Layer 3 | Implementation | Implementation |
| Root | Framework | Framework |
| Sub-layer | Protocols | Protocols |
| Sub-layer | Standards | Standards |

### Body-content terminology (TH)

In Thai **body content** (not nav), layer concepts may take a Thai descriptor where it carries the right altitude:

| Concept | TH body term | Never |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Implementation (doing) | **การนำไปปรับใช้จริง** | ~~การประยุกต์ใช้~~ (deprecated, removed) |
| Framework (root) | Framework | **แก่น** ← BANNED |

**แก่น is never used anywhere on the site, in any copy, label, or CTA.** Even though แก่น is the precise Thai word for "essence/core," it is too archaic and cognitively heavy for UX. **การประยุกต์ใช้ is deprecated** — Implementation body content uses **การนำไปปรับใช้จริง** ("putting into real applied use"), which carries the doing/applied-in-reality altitude.

### Mixing Thai + English in copy

Mixing Thai verbs with English nouns is **natural and accepted** in Thai digital UX. These patterns are approved:

| TH copy | English equivalent | Notes |
|---------|-------------------|-------|
| `อ่าน Framework` | Read Framework | Thai verb + English noun — natural |
| `สำรวจ Journal` | Explore Journal | Thai verb + English noun — natural |
| `สำรวจ Journal →` | Explore Journal → | natural |
| `หรือสำรวจ Journal` | Or Explore Journal | natural |
| `อ่าน Framework` | Read Framework | natural |

**When to use Thai vs English for a term:**
- If the English word is more recognizable to Thai readers in a digital/tech context → use English
- If a natural Thai word exists and is equally clear → use Thai
- If the Thai word is formal, archaic, or cognitively heavy → use English (e.g., แก่น → Framework)

### Attribution and authorial voice

Paritsea content is signed by the author's name alone. No source label, document name, or framework title is appended to attributions — the page context makes the origin clear.

**Canonical attribution format:**
- EN: `— Parit Ritchai`
- TH: `— ปาริศ ฤทธิ์ชัย`

**Never:** `— Parit Ritchai, Framework` / any variant that names a source document. The reader already knows they are in Paritsea.

This rule applies to: closing quotes, pull-quotes, any attributed statement across the site.

**Phase 4 implication:** All UX copy that attributes a voice or quote must use name-only attribution. Source labelling belongs in metadata (publish date, category), not in the attribution line.

## Principles for long-term coherence

1. **One lens, one voice.** All content sounds like one person reading the world — not a committee, not a curation.
2. **Altitude discipline.** Every piece lives at its correct layer and does not borrow from neighbors.
3. **Depth over reach.** No content is produced to optimize volume or discoverability. Content is produced when there is something worth naming.
4. **Immutability at the root.** The Framework does not evolve under publishing pressure. It is the stable base.
5. **Bridges, not merges.** Cross-layer connections are links, not absorbed content.
6. **Resonance before conversion.** The goal of any piece is that a reader recognizes something they felt but could not say — not that they click a CTA.
7. **The page taxonomy is closed.** Future content is routed to existing pages at existing altitudes. New page types require a new foundational decision, not a convenience addition.

## Foundational sentence for all future decisions

> *Does this decision help the reader see what was invisible — without flattening the depth, forcing a sequence, or borrowing a neighboring altitude?*

If yes, it is aligned with Paritsea.
If no, it is drift — and drift compounds over time.

## Language as Cognitive Architecture

Paritsea treats language as structural cognition, not communication.

Language is divided into three operational layers:

1. Entry Vocabulary — immediate comprehension
2. Gateway Vocabulary — cognitive reframing
3. Foundational Ontology — structural definition of thought

This hierarchy ensures that understanding is not static, but progressively reconstructed over time.

The system does not aim to simplify thought.
It aims to evolve the structure of thought through controlled linguistic exposure.
